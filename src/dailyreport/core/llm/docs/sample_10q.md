# PART I - FINANCIAL INFORMATION

## Item 1. Financial Statements

### Consolidated Balance Sheets

Total assets increased to $45.2 billion compared to $42.1 billion in the prior year period. Cash and cash equivalents were $12.3 billion at quarter end.

Current liabilities decreased to $8.9 billion from $9.4 billion. Long-term debt remained stable at $15.2 billion.

### Consolidated Statements of Income

Revenue for Q3 2024 was $18.7 billion, an increase of 8% year-over-year. Gross profit was $7.8 billion, representing a gross margin of 41.7%.

Operating expenses were $4.2 billion. Research and development spending increased 12% to $1.8 billion. Sales and marketing expenses were $1.5 billion.

Net income was $2.9 billion, or $4.52 per diluted share, compared to $2.6 billion, or $3.98 per diluted share, in Q3 2023.

## Item 2. Management's Discussion and Analysis of Financial Condition and Results of Operations

### Overview

Our company delivered strong results in Q3 2024, driven by robust demand across all segments. We continue to execute on our strategic priorities while navigating macroeconomic uncertainties.

The technology sector remains competitive, but our investments in AI and cloud infrastructure are positioning us well for future growth. Customer adoption of our new platform exceeded expectations.

### Results of Operations

Revenue growth was driven primarily by our Cloud Services segment, which grew 23% year-over-year. Our traditional Hardware segment declined 3%, consistent with broader market trends.

Gross margin improved 150 basis points due to favorable product mix and manufacturing efficiencies. We expect margins to remain stable in Q4.

Operating margin was 19.3%, up from 17.8% in the prior year. This improvement reflects operating leverage and disciplined cost management.

### Liquidity and Capital Resources

We generated $3.2 billion in operating cash flow during the quarter. Free cash flow was $2.4 billion after capital expenditures of $800 million.

We returned $1.5 billion to shareholders through dividends and share repurchases. Our board authorized an additional $5 billion share repurchase program.

We maintain a strong balance sheet with investment-grade credit ratings. Our debt-to-EBITDA ratio is 1.2x, well within our target range.

## Item 3. Quantitative and Qualitative Disclosures About Market Risk

Our exposure to market risk includes interest rate risk and foreign currency risk.

Interest rate risk: A 100 basis point increase in rates would reduce the fair value of our debt portfolio by approximately $120 million.

Foreign currency risk: Approximately 40% of our revenue is denominated in currencies other than USD. We use hedging instruments to mitigate this exposure.

## Item 4. Controls and Procedures

Management evaluated the effectiveness of our disclosure controls and procedures as of the end of the period covered by this report. Based on this evaluation, our CEO and CFO concluded that our disclosure controls and procedures were effective.

There were no changes in our internal control over financial reporting during the quarter that materially affected our internal controls.
